PSHS-CARC Mixed Martial Arts (MMA) Club
Annual Curriculum & Training Program

Introduction

Welcome to the official training curriculum of the PSHS-CARC Mixed Martial Arts (MMA) Club. Designed specifically for the scholars of the Philippine Science High School, this program balances the rigorous demands of academic excellence with the physical, mental, and emotional benefits of martial arts.

Our curriculum is built upon three foundational pillars: Discipline, Self-Defense, and Camaraderie. Recognizing that our scholars may encounter challenges both inside and outside the classroom, this program prioritizes grappling arts (Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu and Judo) in the first half of the year. This equips smaller defenders with high-leverage, non-concussive techniques to safely neutralize larger, untrained aggressors.

In the second half of the year, scholars are introduced to striking fundamentals (Boxing and Wushu Sanda) to develop distance management, timing, and well-rounded self-defense capabilities. By stepping onto these mats, scholars commit to embodying the PSHS Core Values of Integrity, Excellence, and Service to the Nation (IES) in every drill, spar, and interaction.

Standard Session Structure (90 Minutes)

Due to the demanding academic schedule and the cold Baguio climate, sessions strictly adhere to the following format to maximize learning and prevent injury:

  • 00–15 mins: Warm-up & Mobility – Breakfalls, shrimping, sprawling, and dynamic stretching to generate heat and prep joints.
  • 15–60 mins: Technique Instruction & Drilling – Three connected techniques taught in focused 15-minute blocks.
  • 60–80 mins: Positional / Light Flow Sparring – Progressive resistance drills focusing on safety and application.
  • 80–90 mins: Cool Down & Debrief – Stretching, reiterating club principles, and a formal bow-out.

Quarter 1: Grappling Fundamentals & Early Submissions

Focus: Surviving takedowns, distance management, and applying high-leverage joint locks and chokes to quickly neutralize an attacker.

WeekTopic / Techniques
Week 1Distance Management, Technical Stand-Up & Run, and Judo Breakfalls (Ukemi).
Week 2Wrist/Arm Grab Escapes, Standing Guillotine Choke (application and control), and Defending the Standing Front Choke.
Week 3Safe Entry to the Clinch, Body Lock Takedown, and Scarf Hold (Kesa Gatame) control.
Week 4Mount Survival (T-Rex arms), The Upa Escape (Bridge & Roll), and the Americana Armlock (from top mount/side control).
Week 5Closed Guard Posture, Breaking Posture, and the Kimura Lock (from bottom guard).
Week 6Rear Naked Choke (RNC) mechanics, Defending the RNC (hand fighting), and Taking the Back from a standing arm drag.
Week 7Escaping the Grounded Headlock/Bull Choke, Framing Away, and the Elbow-Knee Escape (from mount).
Week 8Q1 Review. Positional sparring starting from dominant/inferior ground positions to test early submission retention.

Quarter 2: Advanced Ground Control & Takedown Defense

Focus: Deepening the BJJ base, advancing takedowns, and using the legs for defense and offense.

WeekTopic / Techniques
Week 1O-Soto-Gari (Outside leg trip), Defending the Trip, and Passing the Guard (Torreando).
Week 2Single Leg Takedown, Defending the Single Leg (The Whizzer), and Sprawling.
Week 3Side Control Survival, Shrimping to Guard, and Turning to the Knees to stand up.
Week 4Armbar from the Mount, Armbar from Guard, and Defending the Armbar (stacking).
Week 5Triangle Choke Basics (using the legs to trap a larger opponent) and Defending the Triangle.
Week 6The Scissor Sweep and The Hip Bump Sweep (reversing position to get on top).
Week 7Push-offs/Upkicks (creating space) and Technical Stand-Up against a standing opponent.
Week 8Q2 Review. "Shark Tank" grappling survival drills (defending bottom positions and fighting for submissions).

Quarter 3: Striking Fundamentals & MMA Blending

Focus: Introducing Boxing and Wushu Sanda, learning to defend strikes, and safely transitioning from striking to the clinch.

WeekTopic / Techniques
Week 1MMA Stance & Footwork, The Jab, and The Cross (Straight Rear).
Week 2The Slip (head movement), Countering off the Slip, and Covering Up (Helmet Guard).
Week 3Roundhouse Kick (Low/Mid), Checking Kicks, and The Teep (Push Kick for distance).
Week 4Striking into the Clinch (Jab-Cross to Body Lock) and Defending Strikes from the Clinch.
Week 5Catching a Kick and Sweeping the Standing Leg (Wushu style).
Week 6Defending Ground-and-Pound from Guard (clinching the head) and Sweeping a Striking Opponent.
Week 7Ground-and-Pound Mechanics (controlled strikes from top mount) and Sprawl to Taking the Back against a striker.
Week 8Q3 Review. Light MMA flow sparring (10-20% speed, focusing on transitioning between striking and grappling).

Quarter 4: Scenario Defense & Full Integration

Focus: Putting all the pillars together under pressure, focusing on real-world scenarios and embodying PSHS-CARC excellence.

WeekTopic / Techniques
Week 1Jab-Cross to Double Leg Takedown and Passing the Guard immediately.
Week 2Wall/Locker Defense: Pressing an opponent to the wall, turning off the wall, and safely disengaging.
Week 3Multi-Phase Escape: Taking strikes, moving from the ground to the feet, and verbal de-escalation.
Week 4Defending the Sucker Punch (reaction drills) into a Standing Guillotine or Body Lock.
Week 5Chaining Submissions: Transitioning from a failed Kimura into a Bump Sweep or Triangle.
Week 6Situational Sparring: Starting against the wall, starting mounted, or starting with an opponent holding a clinch.
Week 7Final "Shark Tank" Drills. 1 minute striking, 1 minute clinch, 1 minute ground survival against rotating partners.
Week 8Final Mat Evaluation. Comprehensive open mat, reflection on the core values of Integrity, Excellence, and Service applied to martial arts.
